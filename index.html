Upload your hourly usage and this tool runs it against every residential rate plan — basic charges, riders, and all — to tell you which one is actually cheapest for the way you use electricity.

First

Get your usage file from Georgia Power

Log into georgiapower.com → Menu → Billing and Payments → Usage → select Hourly on the chart → click the Chart Options button and select Switch to kWh View → on desktop click the Export dropdown to the right and select Export to Excel, on moble click Chart Options again and click Export Excel → choose custom date range (most recent 12+ months recommended) → then click the Export button. The file should have columns labeled Hour and kWh.

Drag it onto the box below, or click to pick it from your computer. Everything runs locally in your browser — nothing gets uploaded to any server.

Why each plan costs what it does

About this tool. This is an unofficial calculator built from Georgia Power's published 2025/2026 tariff schedules and the Georgia PSC bill calculator. Rates included: basic service charge ($0.4603/day), Fuel Cost Recovery (4.59¢ summer / 4.29¢ winter per kWh), Environmental Compliance Cost Recovery (13.23% of base bill), and the DSM-R rider (1.22% of base bill). Municipal franchise fees (~3–4%) and state/local sales tax (~7–8%) are excluded because they apply equally to every plan and don't change which plan is cheapest — your real bill will be modestly higher than the totals shown here, but the ranking and the savings between plans are accurate.

Caveats. Plan rates change via PSC filings — verify on the Georgia Power rate plans page before switching. FlatBill and Pay-by-Day are customized per household and not modeled here. TOU plans technically use a separate Time-of-Use Fuel Cost Recovery schedule with values close to the standard FCR used here. The Smart Usage demand charge is calculated from your highest single hour each month, which assumes hourly meter granularity matches the actual 60-minute demand window.

Privacy. Your usage file is parsed entirely in your browser. Nothing is uploaded to any server.
